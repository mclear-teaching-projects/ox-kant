Week 6 - Introspection as Inner Sense

Date Wed 31 May 2017 Tags introspection / inner sense / self-consciousness / self-knowledge

Kant famously characterizes our introspective awareness of our states as, following Locke, a kind of sense—viz. “inner” sense. This week we’ll look at two issues. First, the issue of whether, and to what extent, inner sense is really a form of sense. Second, whether, and to what extent, inner sense differs from “outer” sense in the what and how of its presentation of things.

Core Readings

  • Locke: excerpts from Essay Concerning Human Understanding
  • Kant: excerpts from the Aesthetic (on time), A-Deduction (especially §3 A103-110), and B-Deduction (especially §§16-17, 24)
  • McLear, Kant on the Parity of Inner & Outer Sense
  • Handout

Recommended Readings

  • Geach, The Notion of ‘Inner Sense’
  • Valaris, Inner Sense, Self-Affection, & Temporal Consciousness in Kant’s Critique of Pure Reason
  • Shoemaker, Self-Knowledge and “Inner Sense”: Lecture I
